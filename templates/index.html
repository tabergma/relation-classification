Relation Extraction



Welcome! In the following you are ask to classify relations as correct or incorrect, in order to evaluate the system I developed in my master thesis. Before you start, please read the following definition carefully.

Definition of the term 'relation'

A relation extraction is defined as tuple t = (ei, rj, ek), where ei and ek are entities and rj is the relation between them. ei and ej should be noun phrases, whereas rj should be some verb phrase. A relation can occur between any nouns.
A relations should also be
  • Informative: An informative relation does not omit critical information.
  • Coherent: The extracted relation phrase (rj) should have a meaningful interpretation.
  • Factual: A relation should express a fact and if needed also the circumstances under which this fact holds.

Examples
Sentence 1
Microsoft kaufte LinkedIn für mehr als 26 Millionen US-Dollar im Juni 2016.
Possible relation extraction:
(Microsoft, kaufte LinkedIn für mehr als 26 Millionen US-Doller im, Juni 2016)
The relation is not correct. The relation phrase is too specific and has no meaningful interpretation (incoherent).

Sentence 2
Microsoft macht einen Deal mit LinkedIn.
Possible relation extraction:
(Microsoft, macht, einen Deal)
The relation is uninformative. The fact with whom the deal was made is missing. The correct relation would be:
(Microsoft, macht einen Deal mit, LinkedIn)

Sentence 3
John Doe behauptet, dass Microsoft LinkedIn gekauft hat.
Possible relation extraction:
(Microsoft, hat gekauft, LinkedIn)
The relation itself is correct, but it is not factual. The relation is only true with the additional information, that someone is claiming this purchase.


Your Task

In the following you see one sentence at a time. The relations, which are extracted from this sentence, are also shown. You must mark each relation as correct (YES) or incorrect (NO). If the relation is incorrect, you can also specify why: Is the relation informative, incoherent or/and non-factual? If nothing of those reasons is selected, we imply that the relation itself is simply wrong.

You can stop the classification at any time. You are told at which sentence you should start your next session. Therefore, you just enter the number of that sentence in the field below.
